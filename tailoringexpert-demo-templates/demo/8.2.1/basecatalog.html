DLR - AeroSpace Quality Industry Cooperation

Tailoring Catalogue – In General

The tailoring catalogue is prepared by a working group composed by competent representatives from important prime space contractorsand DLR Product Assurance experts. They release the main issues of this catalogue. Since 2018 there is a "Arbeitskreis DLR RFMPA/QA Anforderungen" established that introduces minor updates and additions to keep the catalogue in an up-to-date status. For this revisions of the signed issue are released. For improved transparency it was decided in March 2019 by the Arbeitskreis to revise the sub-title of the catalogue to "ProductAssurance, Safety & Sustainability" to reflect that the requirement chapters starting with chapter 11 are not purley PA/QA and Safety related but releated to sustainability topics.

DLR Space Administration Tailoring Methodology

The DLR Space Administration Tailoring Methodology is based on the type of mission or project in combination with the corresponding specific risk assessments.
As first step the project type has to be identified. For this purpose several characteristics of missions or projects in national space program have to distinguished, i.e. Space Flight Type (e.g., satellite, lander), Launcher Type (e.g., expendable, reusable), Utilization (e.g., freeflyer, ISS internal), Objective (spacecraft, payload), Maintainability Requirements level, Environment Conditions (atmosphere, space), Safety Requirements level, and Manned Flight Authority (ESA, NASA, Roscosmos). These characteristics specify the project sufficiently to identify which project type needs to be applied in the tailoring process, i.e. SAT, INST, ISS and MO. In the second step of the tailoring methodology, these mission or project characteristics are complemented with refined analyses regarding lifetime, budget class, complexity, technology risk, and risk policy levels (high, low) according the definitions given below.
  • Lifetime
    Required Lifetime of operational capability.The total period of time in a system's life that it is operational and meeting the user's need.
  • Budget
    Total budget allocated for the project.
  • Complexity: High
    The effort concerning adaption, interfaces and/or integration will be important.
  • Complexity: Low
    Low complex system with plain and knowing adaption and interfaces.
  • Technology Risk: High
    For the realisation of the product the effort concerning: available technical materials, performance parameter, performance load conditions, environmental conditions will be important
  • Technology Risk: Low
    For the realisation of the product proven technology that are state of the art are available and can be applied.
  • Risk Policy: Priority High
    A project when loss of the mission would be unacceptable concerning space programme activities or provider of services
  • Risk Policy: Priority Low
    A minimum cost project. The mission is only worthwhile if its cost is kept down. The management activities are reduced to a minimum. The level of accepted risk is higher.
Based on the these risk assessments the requirement levels are determined for each of the fields Product Assurance Management, Quality Assurance Management, Maintainability, Dependability, Safety/Space Debris Mitigation, EEE Parts Procurement, Software Assurance and Materials, Mechanicals Parts and Processes.
